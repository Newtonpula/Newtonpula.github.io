Introduction

Origami is a unique artistic form, not only from the aesthetic view, but also from the pragmatism view. It serves as an inspiration for scientists. However, there is a scarcity of people knowing modern origami and little research provides a comprehensive introduction to the history of origami and its develop nowadays. Thus, our research contains an introduction to origami and its application in the realm of engineering and science.

Origami is an ancient handicraft which could be traced back to Song Dynasty, China and then developed by the Japanese. Early origami was seen as a symbol of some emotion, like friendship and love, as well as used for sacrifice. Later in the early 1900s, famous Japanese artist Akira Yoshizawa innovated the techniques and, which inspired a renaissance of the art form. Nowadays, origami is evolving rapidly due to the contribution of mathematics theorem and computational mathematics. It has become one of Japan’s national treasures.

Community building of origami begins early but remains inadquate. The one-time prevalence of Baidu Tieba holds much appeal for origami lovers. A branch of Baidu Tieba, namely, Zhezhiba, becomes the biggest community for Chinese origami lovers. However, with the constant recession of Baidu Tieba, Zhezhiba has lost much active users. Another community, Zhezhi College (renamed Zhezhi Daxue in 2018), is at the edge of bankruptcy. Due to the niche nature of modern origami, maintaining a colony for origami lovers is costly.

Applications of origami range from materials to aerospace. Since the basics of origami can be used to create complex structures with small number of elements or with fewer resources, it is an efficient method of utilizing the resources for making the devices.
